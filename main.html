Connection doesn't work

  • Check that the device is powered and the COM port is correct.
  • Verify baud rate and other UART parameters match the device.
  • Make sure no other application is using the same port.

No movement detected

  • Confirm enable pin / driver is active and no error LED is lit.
  • Check that target position or speed is not zero.
  • Look for error/status codes in the Info tab.

Movement is jerky or noisy

  • Reduce acceleration or jerk limits in the motion settings.
  • Check motor wiring order and phase sequence.
  • Ensure supply voltage and current rating meet motor requirements.

Position feedback looks wrong

  • Verify encoder/feedback type and resolution settings.
  • Check for loose encoder connector or damaged cable.
  • Re‑home the axis and compare commanded vs. reported position.

EEPROM values not saved

  • Press “Write/Apply” after editing parameters, then “Save to EEPROM”.
  • Power‑cycle the device and re‑read parameters to verify.
  • Make sure no write‑protect flag or jumper is enabled on the board.

Overcurrent or fault error

  • Check motor and power wiring for shorts or reversed polarity.
  • Reduce current limit settings in the driver configuration.
  • Allow the driver to cool down and ensure heatsinking is adequate.

Voltage/temperature values look unrealistic

  • Check the sensor wiring and reference connections.
  • Confirm ADC scaling, gain, and offset parameters in settings.
  • Compare with an external multimeter or thermometer to cross‑check.

Script does not start or stops immediately

  • Make sure the device is connected and not in an error state.
  • Check the log/console for syntax errors in the script.
  • Verify safety limits (position, current, temperature) are not triggered at start.
